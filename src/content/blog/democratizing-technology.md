## The Problem: Technology as a Black Box

Most small and mid-size businesses treat technology as a necessary expense they do not fully understand. They buy tools on vendor recommendations, stack SaaS subscriptions without auditing overlap, and pay premium rates for solutions they barely use.

The average small business uses 40 to 100 SaaS applications. Many of those overlap. Some sit completely unused while the credit card keeps getting charged. And the business owner? They have no idea, because nobody ever sat down with them and asked the simple question: "What do you actually use, and why?"

## What Gets Covered

Every business is different, so every session is tailored. But here are the areas I typically explore:

**Current Tools and Software.** What are you paying for? What are you actually using? Where do tools overlap or go unused?

**Security Basics.** Password hygiene, two-factor authentication, backups, access control. The fundamentals that most businesses skip until something goes wrong.

**Automation Opportunities.** Manual processes that eat hours every week. Data entry, email sequences, reporting, file management. Many of these can be automated with tools you already have.

**Vendor Lock-in Risks.** Are you stuck with a provider because migration seems impossible? What happens if they raise prices? Are there better alternatives?

**AI Readiness.** Where could AI realistically help your business right now? Not the hype, not the buzzwords. Practical applications that save time and reduce errors.
